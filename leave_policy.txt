COMPANY HR LEAVE POLICY DOCUMENT
Version 2.1 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024

=============================================================
SECTION 1: TYPES OF LEAVE
=============================================================

1.1 CASUAL LEAVE (CL)
- Employees are entitled to 12 days of casual leave per calendar year.
- Casual leave cannot exceed 3 consecutive days at a time.
- Casual leave cannot be carried forward to the next year.
- Casual leave requires prior intimation to the reporting manager at least 1 day in advance.
- Casual leave cannot be combined with sick leave without HR approval.
- Half-day casual leave is permitted with manager approval.

1.2 SICK LEAVE (SL)
- Employees are entitled to 12 days of sick leave per calendar year.
- Sick leave of up to 2 days does not require medical documentation.
- Sick leave exceeding 2 consecutive days requires a valid medical certificate from a registered doctor.
- Sick leave must be reported to the manager on the first day of absence, preferably before 10:00 AM.
- Sick leave can be carried forward up to a maximum of 30 days.
- Sick leave cannot be taken as planned leave; it is only for genuine medical emergencies.

1.3 EARNED LEAVE / PRIVILEGE LEAVE (EL/PL)
- Employees accrue 1.5 days of earned leave per month, totaling 18 days per year.
- Earned leave requires prior approval from the reporting manager at least 7 days in advance.
- A minimum of 3 days of earned leave must be taken at a time.
- Earned leave can be carried forward up to 45 days maximum.
- Earned leave encashment is permitted up to 15 days per year during annual leave settlement.
- Earned leave cannot be availed during the probation period (first 6 months).

1.4 MATERNITY LEAVE
- Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks (182 days) of paid maternity leave.
- Maternity leave requires medical documentation and advance notice of at least 4 weeks.
- Maternity leave is applicable after completion of 80 days of work in the preceding 12 months.

1.5 PATERNITY LEAVE
- Male employees are entitled to 5 days of paid paternity leave.
- Paternity leave must be taken within 30 days of the child's birth.
- Paternity leave requires submission of birth certificate.

1.6 BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
- Employees are entitled to 5 days of paid bereavement leave for immediate family members.
- Immediate family includes: spouse, children, parents, and siblings.
- Additional 2 days unpaid leave may be granted for extended family members.
- Bereavement leave must be reported to HR within 24 hours.

1.7 COMPENSATORY OFF (COMP-OFF)
- Comp-off is granted when employees work on weekends or public holidays.
- Comp-off must be utilized within 30 days of the working day.
- Unused comp-off lapses after 30 days and cannot be encashed.

=============================================================
SECTION 2: LEAVE APPLICATION & APPROVAL PROCESS
=============================================================

2.1 APPROVAL HIERARCHY
- Up to 3 days: Reporting Manager approval required.
- 4 to 7 days: Reporting Manager + Department Head approval required.
- More than 7 days: Reporting Manager + Department Head + HR approval required.
- Maternity/Paternity leave: HR approval directly.

2.2 APPLICATION PROCEDURE
- All leave applications must be submitted through the HR portal.
- Emergency leave may be communicated via phone or email and formalized within 24 hours.

=============================================================
SECTION 3: LEAVE RESTRICTIONS
=============================================================

3.1 GENERAL RESTRICTIONS
- No more than 2 employees from the same team can be on leave simultaneously.
- Leave cannot be taken during project deadlines unless approved by the Director.
- Employees on probation are not eligible for earned leave.
- Leave without pay (LWP) is only granted after all applicable leave balances are exhausted.

3.2 COMBINATION RULES
- Casual leave and sick leave cannot be combined.
- Earned leave can be combined with casual leave with manager approval.
- Public holidays sandwiched between leave days are counted as leave days.

3.3 NOTICE PERIOD RULES
- Casual leave during notice period requires manager and HR approval.
- Sick leave during notice period requires medical documentation regardless of duration.
- Earned leave encashment is offered during full and final settlement.
